# Glingu — A Friendly Guide for the Teacher

Hi 👋 — this is the plain-language version of the plan. No technical background needed.
Alexis handles all the building; this document is so **you** can stay an informed
decision-maker without having to learn the jargon. There's a glossary at the end —
whenever you hit a strange word, it's probably explained there.

**Last updated:** 2026-06-04

---

## The big idea (in one breath)

We're building your website **in small, useful steps** — and we start by making it
able to **earn money as early as possible**, using simple, ready-made tools. The
fancy "students log in and take whole courses" platform comes **much later**, only
once the simple version is already working and paying off.

Think of it like teaching a language: we don't start with subjunctive essays. We
start with "hello, how much, when's the class?" — and build up from there.

---

## What we're building first: the landing page

A **landing page** is your shop window on the internet — one beautiful page that
explains who you are and what you offer, in **Spanish and English** (with a button
to switch between them, and more languages later).

It will have:

- A short **video** of you.
- A clear message about why someone should learn with you.
- Your **offerings**: courses, group classes, and official certification prep.
- A quick **placement test** so visitors can find their level.
- A way for interested people to **leave their email** or **pay** for a class.

The clever part: people will be able to **pay you and book classes** through this
page using trusted, ready-made tools — *before* we build anything complicated.
That means money can start coming in early.

---

## What comes later: the student platform

Eventually (only when the simple version is working) we'll add the "members area":

1. **Group classes** — students subscribe, pay automatically each month, and see
   their upcoming classes with a "Join" button. The class link and calendar invite
   are created **automatically** — you never have to email links by hand again.
   It uses **the same Google Meet and Google Calendar you already use.**
2. **Courses & certification prep** — students buy a course and get access to the
   lessons (videos and PDFs can live in Google Drive or YouTube, like you do now).
3. **Placement tests inside the site.**
4. **A content manager** — a simple admin screen where new courses and classes can
   be added. This is the **biggest** piece, so we build it **last**, only when
   doing it by hand becomes a real burden.

---

## What stays exactly as you like it

You keep working the way you already do:

- **Google Meet** for classes.
- **Google Drive** for your materials.
- **Google Calendar** for scheduling.

The website plugs into these — it doesn't replace your habits.

---

## Your job vs. our job

- **You:** focus on your craft — teaching, your expertise, your voice. When we need
  the *real* words (your story, how you describe a course, your testimonials), we'll
  ask you, because nobody can fake a linguist's copy.
- **Alexis:** everything technical, plus writing and polishing the website text
  (in both languages) with your input.

You don't have to learn any of the technical tools. You just have to be the expert
on **the teaching and the message.**

---

## One honest expectation to set

You mentioned having video classes happen *inside* the website. Google doesn't
allow its Meet calls to be placed inside another website — that's a Google rule, not
a budget choice. So instead, the site will **create the Meet link and the calendar
invite automatically** and give students a **"Join class"** button. Same result for
the student, none of the cost and delay of building our own video system. If we ever
truly need calls built into the site, that's a "someday, if it's worth it" item.

---

## What it costs (roughly)

To **start**, the running costs are close to **zero** — the tools we use are free at
small scale. The only ongoing costs are:

- **Google Workspace** (your professional email + tools): a few euros a month.
- **Payment processing**: a small percentage **only when you actually get paid**
  (the company that handles cards takes a cut per sale).

The real investment is **time**, which is exactly why we build in small steps and
earn early.

---

## How we'll work, step by step

1. **Set up the basics** and get a simple "coming soon" page live.
2. **Build the full landing page** (both languages) and start collecting interested
   emails.
3. **Turn on payments** so people can buy a class or course.
4. **Add the on-site placement test.**
5. Only *then* start the student platform — beginning with **group classes**,
   because that's the most reliable income.

You'll always know which step we're on, and each step produces something real and
useful — never months of invisible work.

## Glossary (plain meanings)

| Term you might hear | What it actually means |
|---|---|
| **Landing page** | The single welcome page of your website — your shop window. |
| **Localization / i18n** | Offering the site in multiple languages (Spanish, English, more). |
| **Language switcher** | The button that flips the site between languages. |
| **CTA ("call to action")** | A button or link that asks the visitor to do something — "Book a class," "Take the test." |
| **Hosting** | The service that keeps the website online, like rent for a shop space. |
| **Domain** | Your web address (e.g. yourname.com). |
| **Framework** | The set of building tools we use to make the site — like a kitchen's equipment. |
| **Stripe** | The trusted company that safely handles card payments. We never touch card numbers. |
| **Payment Link** | A ready-made Stripe page that lets someone pay you — no custom software needed. |
| **Supabase** | Where the site securely stores accounts and data (later, for the student platform). |
| **Auth / log in** | The system that lets students sign in safely. We use a ready-made, secure one. |
| **API** | A way for two services to talk to each other automatically (e.g. our site asking Google Calendar to make a class). |
| **Calendar invite / .ics** | The little file that makes an event pop into someone's calendar automatically. |
| **Google Meet integration** | The site automatically creating your Meet links instead of you doing it by hand. |
| **MDX / content** | The format we write your website's text in. You give the words; we format them. |
| **CMS / content manager** | A future admin screen to add courses yourself. We build it last. |
| **LMS** | "Learning Management System" — the full students-take-courses platform. The long-term goal. |
| **SEO** | Making sure Google can find and rank your site so new students discover you. |
| **GDPR** | EU privacy rules — why the site will have a privacy policy and a cookie notice. |
| **Deploy / live** | "Putting it online so the world can see it." |
